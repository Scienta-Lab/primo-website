The patient, not the cell, is what a drug has to treat.

Why PRIMO

Omics foundation models need their CASP.

AI for drug development has no shared way to compare models on the outcomes that matter. Every paper brings its own tasks and evaluation, making progress difficult to measure.

PRIMO is building a standardized, community-driven protocol: the same tasks, metrics, and honest statistical baselines for every model.

01

Real stakes

Real diseases, patient-level endpoints, and questions spanning discovery, translation, and clinical application.

02

Real omics conditions

High dimensionality, batch effects, multiple modalities, and the data-limited reality of clinical cohorts.

03

Honest baselines

Every foundation model is measured against strong statistical baselines—even when the simpler method wins.

The benchmark

One reference line,
from discovery to clinic.

PRIMO begins in immunology and inflammation with transcriptomic tasks, and is designed to grow across modalities and therapeutic areas.

01

Discovery

Can a model identify biology worth pursuing?

  • Target efficacy
  • Gene function
02

Preclinical translation

Does signal transfer across experimental boundaries?

  • Cross-species translation
  • Cross-disease perturbation
03

Clinical application

Can it help predict outcomes for patients?

  • Treatment response
  • Patient stratification
  • Disease activity

Built for comparison, not promotion. PRIMO aims to tell signal from noise and establish shared best practices for robust model evaluation.
